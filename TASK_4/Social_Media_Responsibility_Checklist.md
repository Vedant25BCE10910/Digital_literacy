The Do's (Build Your Brand)
* Audit Your Privacy Monthly: Treat your privacy settings like a subscription’┐Įcheck them regularly. Platforms update their terms often, and what was private last semester might not be today.
* Showcase Your "Wins": Use your feed to document the journey. Post about a successful hackathon, a club event you helped organize, or even a difficult coding problem you finally solved. It builds a "passive resume."
* Network with Intent: When reaching out to professors or industry professionals on LinkedIn, always include a short, personalized note. It turns a cold request into a real connection.
* Engage with Curiosity: Follow leaders in your field and actually comment on their posts. Asking a smart question on a professional thread is one of the fastest ways to get noticed by recruiters.
* Keep the "Grandma Rule" in Mind: Before hitting post, ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable with my favorite professor or a future boss seeing this?" if the answer is "maybe," skip it.
The Don'ts (Protect Your Future)
* Avoid the "Rant" Trap: It’┐Įs tempting to vent about a frustrating professor, a teammate who isn't doing their share, or a campus policy. However, digital venting often looks like a lack of professionalism to outsiders.
* Don't Overshare Your Location: For safety and privacy, avoid posting your exact location in real-time (like your hostel or a specific classroom). Wait until you've left the spot to post that story.
* Don't Neglect Your Digital Shadow: Never assume "deleted" means "gone." Screenshots exist, and archives happen. If you wouldn't want it projected on a lecture screen, don't upload it.
* Skip the Comment Section Wars: It is incredibly easy to get sucked into heated debates. Remember that your digital footprint includes every "like" and "reply"’┐Įkeep your interactions constructive, not combative.
